ABOUT

The objective is to design and construct a contraption, which is a complex mechanism consisting of multiple interconnected steps that occur in a specific sequence to achieve a desired outcome.

In this case, the desired outcome is to make an object, for example a toy or a paper airplane, fly or become airborne. To achieve this outcome, your contraption will need to include several steps that are triggered by the preceding one through appropriate energy conversions.

Each step in the contraption should be designed to contribute to the final goal of getting the object airborne. For example, the first step might involve using a spring to release a lever, which then triggers a fan to blow air onto the object. The second step might involve a mechanism to create a low-pressure zone around the object, providing lift.

As each step is activated by the previous one, the energy from the initial action will be transformed and used to power the next step in the sequence. This process will continue until the final step, where the object is released and becomes airborne.

To achieve the goal of having the object remain in flight for at least three seconds, you may need to design additional steps into your contraption that help stabilize the flight, maintain lift, or adjust the angle of the object in the air.

Overall, creating a contraption to achieve this task requires careful planning, attention to detail, and a thorough understanding of energy conversion and the principles of flight.
